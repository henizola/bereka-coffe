Ethiopia is one of the biggest producers of Coffee arabica to global market. Ethiopian coffee is very known of its organic in nature and its taste and flavor is very likable globally. Generally, Ethiopian coffee is prepared and supplied to market as Washed and Natural which passes through different preparation steps. Natural processing involves drying freshly picked beans in the sun.It is the most traditional and widely used method of making coffee. Ethiopians would pick the beans in the morning and leave them to dry in the afternoon. Beans that have been naturally processed have a strong fruit and wine flavor. They may taste like cherry, grape, lime, or peach. Their natural process imparts an earthy flavor. If beans are not dried properly, they develop a sour flavor and become too brittle to roast. Wet processing necessitates thorough washing rather than being picked and dried immediately. Immediately after being picked, the coffee beans soak in massive water vats. After cleaning, the beans are placed on rotating beds for 48 to 72 hours. This evenly dries the entire bean. Wet processing is a modern method that creates a more consistent flavor
